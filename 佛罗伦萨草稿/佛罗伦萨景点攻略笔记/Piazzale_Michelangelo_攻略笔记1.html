The square sits on a hill on the south bank of the Arno River. It was designed in the 19th century by Florentine architect Giuseppe Poggi as a tribute to the Renaissance master Michelangelo.
Between 5 and 6 p.m., August offers the perfect time for a sunset stroll. On the terrace facing the Ponte Vecchio, you can sit quietly on the steps and wait. As the sun slowly sinks, golden light bathes the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore, the Ponte Vecchio, and the Arno River, making the entire city look as if it has been gilded. The beauty leaves you speechless, and with street performers singing in the background, love lingers in the Florentine sunset.
