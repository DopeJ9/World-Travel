Recommended Tour Routes

Tour 1: Dunkle Welten (WWII/History)

As the opening chapter, enter the WWII-era air raid shelter. Under the guidance of tour guides, witness surviving wall graffiti and wartime ventilation equipment, and listen to stories of air raid alarms and people seeking refuge.

Artistic Perspective: These wartime graffiti are not only historical witnesses but also artistic expressions of people's emotions from that era. By carefully observing the traces on the walls, you'll discover the artistic representation of complex emotions like fear, hope, and despair.

This section establishes the experiential tone of "survival and fear under war," preparing visitors psychologically for the subsequent deep experiences.

Tour 2: Vom Flakturm zum Tr√ºmmerberg (Anti-Aircraft Tower)

Hike through the massive anti-aircraft tower ruins that staff spent 8,000 hours excavating. Navigate through the seven-story structure remains (three floors open to public), experiencing up close the grandeur and incompleteness of the architecture.

Architectural Art: This anti-aircraft tower showcases the characteristics of Nazi-era architecture‚Äîthe combination of functionality and symbolism. From an architectural perspective, observing its structural design helps understand the technological level and political intentions of that time.

The tour incorporates architectural and military strategy explanations, allowing visitors to understand the madness of the Nazi era from a "war engineering" perspective.

Optional: Tour 3 (Cold War/Nuclear Protection Facilities)

If physical strength and time permit, you can choose to continue exploring Cold War-era nuclear protection bunkers. The display of underground water treatment plants, storage areas, and confined spaces provides an intuitive sense of "nuclear fear" in the Cold War atmosphere.

Historical Comparison: From WWII to the Cold War, from air defense to nuclear defense, this place demonstrates the continuity and discontinuity of German history, serving as an important historical node for understanding modern Germany.
