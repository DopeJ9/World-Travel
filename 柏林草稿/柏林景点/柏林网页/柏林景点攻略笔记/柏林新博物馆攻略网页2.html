Second Floor Ancient Egypt Gallery

Enter the museum's core space.

Nefertiti Bust:

Allow 15-20 minutes to examine closely, feel that gaze spanning three millennia.

She is not only a masterpiece of art, but also a politically controversial artifact.

Then slowly view the gold ornaments, divine statues, and mural fragments, experiencing ancient Egypt's dual world of "life and eternity."

Note the spatial design: Exposed brick walls, soft lighting, deliberately minimalist layout that gives you space for contemplation.

First Floor Prehistory and Early History Section

Slowly examine stone tools and pottery, feeling humanity's early exploration of tools and order.

The burial section is worth lingering in, where urns and reconstructed models evoke thoughts of ancient people's daily life and death rituals.

If you read the text explanations carefully, you'll discover connections between the exhibits and prehistoric sites around Berlin.

Basement Classical Civilization Section

Follow the exhibition sequence to view Greek, Roman, and Etruscan artifacts.

The sculptures and inscriptions are not ornate, but rather remind us: these once belonged to real life.

Take time before the reliefs or inscriptions to contemplate the weight of writing and civilization.

Golden Hat

Another "mysterious artifact" in the entire museum.

Carefully observe the surface patterns and imagine how ancient people used it to record time.

Free Pause and Second Look

The special quality of the Neues Museum lies in its "blank spaces" and "silence."

You can choose to pause before a weathered wall, or return to Nefertiti's gallery to experience the difference of a second gaze.
