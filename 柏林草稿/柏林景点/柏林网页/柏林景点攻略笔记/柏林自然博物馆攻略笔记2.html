‚ú® Tour Route & Experience

  1. Stunning Opening: Diplodocus Fossil
    First, take time to appreciate the dinosaur's magnificence in the main hall, capture some panoramic shots.
    Tell children the story of "this creature walking around Earth millions of years ago."
  2. Deep Exploration: Dinosaur & Paleontology Section
    Not just the Diplodocus, but also carefully examine other dinosaur skulls and fossil details.
    Play "Little Dinosaur Researcher" with children, let them find differences in teeth and claws of different dinosaurs.
  3. Archaeopteryx Fossil
    Special forces version: carefully read the text descriptions to understand bird evolution.
    Play a little game with children: "What's similar between dinosaurs and birds?"
  4. Glass Jar Specimen Collection
    Deeply appreciate various deep-sea fish and strange creatures.
    For children, it's like exploring a "monster laboratory," tell them "these are real living creatures."
  5. Bird Exhibition Area
    Take time to slowly observe and learn about different bird species.
    Give children "treasure hunt tasks": find penguins, flamingos, owls, etc.
  6. Rocks, Meteorites & Crystal Exhibits
    Various crystals and meteorites are beautiful, children will think it's like a "gem store."
    Explain while viewing "where these stones come from."
  7. Mid-tour Break & Picnic Suggestions
    The museum has rest areas, bring some snacks to prevent children from energy crashes.
  8. Finale: Children's Playground Disabledpark
    Play for an hour to completely release energy.
    Swings, slides, sandpits plus special climbing equipment, guaranteed to play until they don't want to leave.

üåü Overall Experience

The four to five-hour "special forces mode" is perfect for seeing all museum exhibits, especially ideal for learning while playing with children.

Although smaller than natural history museums in London or Vienna, it's absolutely worth deep exploration during Berlin independent travel.
