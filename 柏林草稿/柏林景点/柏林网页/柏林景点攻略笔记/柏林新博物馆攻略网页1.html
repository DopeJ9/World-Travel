Neues Museum Berlin

Venue Overview

Perfect for visitors with tight schedules on Museum Island who can only spare 2 hours, first-time Berlin visitors who want to see the "must-see exhibits" without missing the museum's treasures. Approximately 2 hours to quickly browse the main exhibits without detours.

Head Straight to Ancient Egypt Gallery (2nd Floor)

The quietest and most immersive exhibition area in the museum.

Main target: Nefertiti bust - she is the soul of the entire museum and the most controversial exhibit (Egypt has long demanded its return). Her gentle yet determined expression is enough to make you pause for ten minutes.

Others can be quickly browsed: tomb models, mural fragments, daily artifacts - just a brief overview.

Prehistory and Early History Section (1st Floor)

The highlight is the burial area: urns and reconstructed burial site models that quickly convey the "simplicity" of early human life.

Recommended stay: about 15 minutes.

Classical Civilization Section (Basement)

Just look at a few representative sculptures and reliefs, no need to examine each one in detail.

If time is tight, you can skip this section entirely.

Must-see second treasure: Golden Hat - as thin as a cicada's wing, only 0.6mm thick, with patterns that remain undeciphered to this day. Standing in front of the display case, you'll feel a sense of curiosity about this "unsolved mystery."
