Berlin Must-Visit Attractions & Booking Guide
1

Berliner Dom

Baroque Cathedral
Berlin's most magnificent Baroque-style cathedral, located on Museum Island. Features a stunning dome and exquisite interior decoration, with panoramic city views from the top. The cathedral houses the Hohenzollern dynasty crypts and stands as one of Germany's most important religious buildings.
Book Tickets
2

Berliner Fernsehturm (TV Tower)

Observation Tower
Berlin's tallest building and iconic landmark, standing at 368 meters. The observation deck at 203 meters offers 360-degree panoramic views of Berlin. The tower also features a revolving restaurant where you can dine while enjoying stunning city views, making it a perfect spot for photography.
Book Tickets
3

DDR Museum

Interactive History Museum
A unique interactive museum vividly showcasing daily life in East Germany. Visitors can experience an East German apartment, drive a Trabant car simulator, and learn about the social system, culture, and lifestyle of the DDR era. An essential visit for understanding Berlin's history.
Book Tickets
4

Neues Museum

Archaeological Museum
One of the most important museums on Museum Island, housing an extensive collection of Ancient Egyptian artifacts and prehistoric archaeological treasures. The star exhibit is the 3,300-year-old bust of Queen Nefertiti. The museum building itself is a masterpiece, perfectly blending historical and modern design.
Book Tickets
5

Museum für Naturkunde

Natural History Museum
One of Europe's largest natural history museums, housing over 30 million specimens. The museum's crown jewel is the world's tallest mounted dinosaur skeleton—a Brachiosaurus standing at 13.27 meters. Features exquisite animal specimens, mineral exhibits, and biodiversity displays, perfect for the whole family.
Book Tickets
6

Berlin Story Bunker

Historical Exhibition
A museum housed in an authentic WWII air-raid shelter, featuring multimedia exhibitions chronicling Berlin's complete history from its founding to modern times. Highlights include the Nazi era, WWII, and Cold War periods. The well-preserved bunker offers visitors an immersive historical experience.
Book Tickets
