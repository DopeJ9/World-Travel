Piazza di Spagna: An Art Lover's Deep Dive

For art enthusiasts, Piazza di Spagna offers far more than just a photo opportunity. This area has been a hub for artists, writers, and intellectuals for centuries. To truly appreciate its artistic heritage, plan to spend at least 2-3 hours exploring the square and its surroundings.

The Spanish Steps themselves are a masterpiece of urban design, connecting Piazza di Spagna with the church of Trinità dei Monti. The 135 steps were built in the 18th century to link the Spanish Embassy to the church.
Fontana della Barcaccia (Fountain of the Old Boat) at the base of the steps was designed by Pietro Bernini (father of the more famous Gian Lorenzo Bernini) in 1627. Its unique boat shape is said to commemorate a flood that left a boat stranded in the square.

Keats-Shelley House

Located to the right of the steps, this museum commemorates the Romantic poets John Keats and Percy Bysshe Shelley, who lived in the area. Keats died here in 1821. The house contains an extensive collection of manuscripts, letters, and memorabilia.

Opening Hours: Monday-Friday 10:00-13:00 & 14:00-18:00, Saturday 11:00-14:00 & 15:00-18:00

Admission: €6 (adults), €5 (reduced)

Shopping & Boutiques

The area around Piazza di Spagna is known for its luxury shopping. Via dei Condotti is home to high-end fashion houses like Gucci, Prada, and Bulgari.

Typical Store Hours: 10:00-19:30 (Monday-Saturday), some open Sundays 11:00-19:00

Note: Many stores close for a few hours in the afternoon (approximately 13:00-16:00) for the traditional Italian riposo.

Babington's Tea Room, established in 1893, is an English tea room at the base of the Spanish Steps that has been serving tea to visitors for over a century, maintaining its original charm and atmosphere.
The Column of the Immaculate Conception in the square was erected in 1857 to commemorate Pope Pius IX's declaration of the dogma of the Immaculate Conception.

Personal Recommendation: As an art history enthusiast, I highly recommend visiting the Keats-Shelley House and then enjoying tea at Babington's. This combination gives you a real sense of the artistic and literary history that permeates this area. I spent a total of 3 hours exploring all these details.

Recommended visiting time for art lovers: 2-3 hours
