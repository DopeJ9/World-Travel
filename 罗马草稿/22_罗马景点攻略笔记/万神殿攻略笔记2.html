Guide 2: Inside the Pantheon

The highlight of the Pantheon is undoubtedly its dome. With a diameter of 43.3 meters, it was already more than twice the size of the largest existing dome when it was built in 125 AD — a record that stood for over a thousand years.
The dome remains the largest unreinforced masonry dome in the world, meaning there are no supporting beams inside. The height of the dome is also 43.3 meters, equal to its diameter, so if a sphere of the same diameter were placed inside, it would fit perfectly.
At the very top is an 8.9-meter oculus ("the Eye of the Pantheon"), which reduces weight and serves as the only source of light.
I visited at noon, when sunlight streamed through the oculus and illuminated the entrance — in my opinion, the perfect time for photos.
The Pantheon is also home to several notable tombs, the most famous being that of Raphael, crowned with a Madonna and Child sculpture.
