Colline Emiliane - Authentic Emilian Experience in the Heart of Rome

Colline Emiliane

Traditional Emilian Cuisine Since 1931

Stepping into Colline Emiliane feels like being transported to a cozy family trattoria in Emilia-Romagna. This historic restaurant has been serving authentic regional specialties since 1931, and the passion for tradition is palpable in every detail. We spent about 2 hours here enjoying a leisurely lunch, completely immersed in the warm, familial atmosphere.

About Colline Emiliane
Located on via degli Avignonesi, this family-run restaurant has been a Roman institution since the 1960s. The small kitchen near the entrance showcases their commitment to fresh, handmade pasta. The restaurant was a favorite haunt of Fellini and other "Dolce Vita" celebrities in its heyday, and you can still feel that timeless charm today.
Overall Experience
The service was warm and attentive without being intrusive. The atmosphere is cozy and traditional, with wooden accents and photos of the restaurant's history adorning the walls. We particularly enjoyed watching the pasta being made fresh in the open kitchen area. The restaurant was moderately busy on a Thursday lunch service, but we didn't feel rushed at all during our visit.
